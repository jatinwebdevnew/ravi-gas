### Section: CONSERVATION TIPS
Intro: "Is there a way to save cooking LPG? Yes, there is and it's no rocket science. Little adjustments in your cooking style here and there will do the trick. Scroll below to find some important tips on how you can save domestic LPG."

Tips list:
1. **Pressure Cooking:** Pressure cooking saves fuel, especially 20% on rice, 46% on pulses and so on as compared to conventional method of cooking. The saving in time is also very good.
2. **Water Quantity:** The optimum quantity of water saves fuel. If you use surplus amount of water, extra energy is required to evaporate the same.
3. **Low flame:** When the liquid reaches boiling point, switch your stove to low flame as it's enough to keep them boiling. Anything extra will be a wastage of energy.
4. **Soaking:** Soaking some foods will reduce extra fuel usage. For example, soaking chickpeas overnight reduces fuel consumption by 22%.
5. **Wide vessels save fuel:** A visible flame touching the sides of vessel wastes fuel since it gives out heat to the surroundings. But if you cover the flame as much as possible by using a broad vessel, you will save fuel.
6. **Use Lids:** It is a good practice to cover cooking vessels and pans with a lid, as an open vessel loses heat to the atmosphere, which means a waste of fuel.
7. **Use small burners:** A cooking gas stove has a big burner and a small burner. The small burner consumes 6% to 10% less.
8. **Clean the burners:** It is important to clean the burner of your gas range regularly. Soot clogged gas burners increase fuel consumption. Regular maintenance of your stove helps you save fuel.
9. **Clean the vessels:** A coating of undissolved salts is usually found on the insides of kettles and cookers. Even a millimetre thick coating can reduce the flow of heat to the vessel's contents. This increases your fuel consumption by as much as 10%.

Closing: "Follow these 9 tips and find yourselves the difference in the LPG consumption in your kitchen.

### Advantages (4 points)
1. **Cost savings:** LPG is generally cheaper than diesel fuel, which can lead to significant cost savings in the long run. The exact savings will depend on the current fuel prices and availability in your region.
2. **Environmental impact:** LPG is considered a cleaner-burning fuel compared to diesel. It produces lower levels of harmful emissions such as carbon dioxide (CO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), sulfur oxides (SOx), and particulate matter. As a result, converting to LPG can help reduce your vehicle's carbon footprint and contribute to improved air quality.
3. **Performance:** LPG combustion typically produces a smoother and quieter engine operation compared to diesel. The engine can run more quietly, with reduced vibration and noise levels.
4. **Engine longevity:** LPG combustion generally produces less wear and tear on engine components compared to diesel. LPG has higher octane rating and lubrication properties, which can contribute to a longer engine life.

### Benefits of LPG (heading + 4 sub-points)
Intro: "LPG (liquefied petroleum gas) offers several benefits across various applications. Here are some of the key advantages of using LPG:"

1. **Versatility:** LPG is a versatile fuel that can be used for various purposes, including residential, commercial, industrial, and automotive applications. It is commonly used for heating, cooking, powering vehicles, and as a fuel in industrial processes.
2. **Clean Burning:** LPG is a cleaner-burning fuel compared to other fossil fuels such as coal and diesel. It emits lower levels of harmful pollutants such as sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate matter, and greenhouse gases. This makes it an environmentally friendly fuel choice.
3. **Energy Efficiency:** LPG has a high calorific value, which means it provides a significant amount of energy per unit of volume. This high energy efficiency makes it an effective fuel for heating and cooking, resulting in reduced energy consumption and lower fuel costs.
4. **Storage and Transport:** LPG is stored and transported in liquid form, which allows for efficient storage and distribution. It can be easily transported in cylinders or bulk tanks, making it accessible in remote areas where natural gas pipelines are not available.

2. Try and keep the cylinders in a cool place and at a safe distance from sparks, flames and high heat.
3. Avoid using LPG in windy situations and immediately suspend all operations in case of unsafe situations.
4. Put on safety cap and place the LPG cylinder in an open area when suspected valve leakage.
5. Read operating instructions mentioned by the manufacturer carefully before use

### Section: Don'ts
1. Don't keep your LPG cylinder in the horizontal position. The right way to use the cylinder is upright.
2. Keep the cylinder away from any direct or indirect heat.
3. Do not connect the cylinder without checking valve and 'O' ring.
4. Don't let the unused and unconnected LPG cylinders hanging around.
5. Don't let any unauthorized person enter the cylinder area.
6. Don't use battery-operated or electrical devices in and around the LPG shed.
